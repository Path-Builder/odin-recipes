Indian Vegetarian Curried Cauliflower

Indian vegetarian curry is a richly spiced and flavorful dish that showcases the diverse ingredients and aromatic traditions of Indian cuisine. Typically made with a combination of vegetables such as potatoes, peas, cauliflower, carrots, or chickpeas, the curry is simmered in a sauce crafted from onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and a blend of warming spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala. The result is a thick, fragrant stew that balances heat, earthiness, and a touch of sweetness. Depending on the region, the base can be tomato-based, coconut milk-based, or yogurt-based, each offering a unique texture and flavor profile.

Often served with steamed rice or flatbreads like naan or roti, Indian vegetarian curry is a satisfying and wholesome meal that appeals to both vegetarians and meat-eaters alike. It’s not only comforting and hearty but also nutrient-dense, thanks to the variety of vegetables and legumes. Whether it’s a North Indian-style aloo gobi or a South Indian vegetable sambar, each version reflects local ingredients and cooking styles, making Indian vegetarian curry one of the most versatile and beloved dishes in global cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 head cauliflower, broken into small florets
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (15 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • ½ (14.5 ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 2 teaspoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ½ (8 ounce) package cream cheese
  • salt to taste
  • 3 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

Directions:

  1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion to the hot oil and cook until golden brown, about 5 minutes. Add cauliflower and mix into the onion. Continue cooking, stirring every 2 to 3 minutes, for 10 to 15 minutes more. Add garlic and cook 2 more minutes.
  2. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, curry powder, coriander, turmeric, cumin, and cayenne pepper. Cook and stir for 5 more minutes.
  3. Mix cream cheese into the skillet with the vegetables. Continue cooking until cauliflower is tender but still a little crunchy, 5 to 10 minutes more. Season with salt and mix in 2 tablespoons cilantro. Garnish with remaining cilantro before serving.
