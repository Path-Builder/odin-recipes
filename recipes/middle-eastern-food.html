Tabbouleh

Tabbouleh is a refreshing and vibrant Levantine salad made primarily with finely chopped parsley, mint, tomatoes, onions, and soaked bulgur wheat. Dressed simply with olive oil and lemon juice, it’s known for its fresh, zesty flavor and light, fluffy texture. Unlike grain-heavy salads, traditional tabbouleh uses just a small amount of bulgur, letting the herbs be the star of the dish. The combination of juicy tomatoes and lemony dressing balances the earthy, grassy tones of the parsley, making it both aromatic and satisfying.

Often served as part of a mezze platter alongside hummus, baba ghanoush, and pita bread, tabbouleh is not only delicious but also highly nutritious. It’s rich in fiber, antioxidants, and vitamins, especially from the parsley and fresh vegetables. Tabbouleh’s appeal lies in its simplicity, its use of fresh ingredients, and its adaptability—it can be enjoyed as a side, a light main course, or a topping for wraps and sandwiches. This salad remains a staple in Middle Eastern cuisine and continues to gain popularity worldwide as a healthy, plant-based dish.

Ingredients:

  • ¼ cup bulgur
  • ½ cup boiling water
  • 5 ripe tomatoes, finely diced
  • 1 small onion, finely diced
  • 1 cup freshly chopped parsley
  • ¼ cup freshly chopped mint leaves
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • salt to taste

Directions:

  1. Place bulgur in a small mixing bowl. Add boiling water, mix, and cover with a clean towel. Let stand for 1 hour. Drain any excess water.
  2. Combine tomatoes, onion, parsley, mint, lemon juice, olive oil, and salt. Add bulgur; mix well and serve.
